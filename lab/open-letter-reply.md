# Reflection on Justice, Change, and the Power of Voice

Because my kids have brown skin…

Because the American country they live in sees *other Americans* as “other” rather than brother/sister sibling — from the Arctic to the Antarctic…

Because we, right here, blame those back then for what they endured…

Because we can’t stop what’s happening now, just as those then were forced to stand by…

Because I have tuned out to protect my sanity — and body — from the cruelty of speaking out publicly…

Because I don’t want my children to advocate in my silence, risking their bodies and minds for causes often won **ONLY** by the power of the pen…

Because Bryan Andrews [wrote this open letter](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/76U5mX6L6Zw), standing up with his voice, his camera, his music and his skin not as a weapon but a banner for change.

Because I was lied to about America the Country for 41 years and everyone denied it until 2020 then forgot by 2024!

## Dear Brian,

Bless your soul for becoming **WOKE**, my brother. Is the Universe self-correcting allowing folks to wake up? You are not alone. Methods of communication used to be limited; now each of us has a Mega-Smart-Phone.

I would like to say this: if we were alive then, yes, I believe you and others would have stood up and said *this is wrong*. People aren’t evil — systems of power and control are. If all people were evil, there would be far fewer of us in the world.

It hurts my heart to live in a time when what we thought was history feels like the present again. That said, I think I found an answer to the question I used to ask: *how could people back then stand by and say or do nothing?*

The answer came as we drove past ICE/cop with a knee in the back of a fellow in Texas fire-ant-infested clay soil on the side of the highway. Both the victim and passersby were helpless to do anything except say out loud that *shit is wrong*.

For a long time I believed that those who allowed such evil were simply on the wrong side of history — until I found myself on that same wrong side, feeling powerless to do anything meaningful in that moment.

I think people of all heritages hated the evil of their times and worked quietly in whatever ways they could toward change.

So now, in this strange time loop, I find myself wondering: *what is the answer when leadership openly desires those childhood times again?* What do we do when we can’t vote for someone younger because candidates are backed only by big money and big-money parties?
