2025 began with intention.

There were plans. Direction. A sense that we were building toward something.

By spring, disruption had taken over.

The kind that makes you question everything—not just your plans, but your capacity to hold the life you’ve chosen.

And in the middle of that unraveling, unschooling didn’t pause.

There was no clean break. No moment where learning stopped and survival began.

Because for us, they were the same.

While everything around us felt uncertain, my child was still noticing, still asking, still making meaning out of what was happening.

And I had to face something I hadn’t fully understood before:

Unschooling isn’t something we do when life is stable.

It’s something that reveals itself when life is not.


## Spring 2025

By the time spring arrived in Texas, we were already exhausted.

Our home felt like it was closing in on us.

Kids growing, bodies stretching, personalities expanding—but the walls stayed the same.

Shared rooms turned into battlegrounds.
Sleep became scattered.
Even the spaces meant for rest and creativity started colliding.

There was no quiet corner left untouched.

And somewhere in that, I realized something I hadn’t wanted to admit:

I didn’t have space either.

Not to think.
Not to create.
Not even to rest without disrupting someone else.

At 40, I had less privacy than I did at 16.

That realization sat heavy.

Because when you are the one holding space for everyone else… where do you go when you need space too? 

So we made a decision.

Not out of luxury—but necessity.

We needed room to grow.

More land. More breathing space. A different way of living that didn’t keep us pressed up against each other’s limits.

What started as a conversation became a plan.

And that plan quickly became everything.

We listed our home.
We shifted our routines.
We stepped into something bigger, not fully knowing how it would unfold.

And just as things began to move…

Everything else did too.

The kind of shift you don’t plan for.
The kind that doesn’t ask if you’re ready.

But that’s a story for the months that followed.

Because this isn’t just a story about what went wrong.

This is a story about what continued.

About how, even in uncertainty, natural learning didn’t disappear.

It adapted.
It deepened.
It revealed itself in ways I hadn’t seen before.

And over the next 12 months, I’m going to share what that actually looked like.

Not the ideal version.

The real one.
