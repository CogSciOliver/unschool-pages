Unschooling is not the absence of learning. It is the refusal to force learning into school-shaped systems when life itself already offers rich, meaningful ways to grow.

## What it is

At its core, unschooling trusts that children learn deeply through living, asking questions, making things, following interests, and participating in real life.

## What it is not

It is not neglect. It is not doing nothing. It is not abandoning support. The parent role shifts from manager to observer, environment designer, and trusted companion.
